The Ethics of our Project

The ethics of creating websites for people that are unfamiliar with technology

November 1, 2024 Paige F. and Nick C.

When exploring online communities, individuals can make valuable contributions while protecting their well-being and respecting the community’s values. Our goal is to make your online experience safe, positive, and enriching. As you explore, we want to share a few simple ethical guidelines that can help make your online interactions enjoyable and respectful. These are designed to help you stay safe, protect your privacy, and contribute positively to any online community.

Ethical Guidelines for a Positive Online Experience

  • Respectfully Across Platforms
    • Use Respectful Language: Whether you are in a group chat, responding to an email, or commenting on a post, use kind, respectful language.
    • Be Open-Minded: Online communities are diverse, and respecting different perspectives helps everyone feel valued
  • Protect your and other people’s information
    • Do NOT share your passwords or sensitive financial information on any public platform
      • Only enter that information when using trusted services like a bank website
      • Instructions on to identify those trusted services are presented in the “Website Navigation” section
    • Do NOT expose another person’s sensitive information for any reason
      • This is a CRIME, and in certain states you will face fines and up to 6 months in county jail
  • Avoid Phone Scams!
    • Do NOT answer unknown numbers
      • If you don’t recognize the number, it’s often safest to let it go to voicemail. Legitimate callers will leave a message, while many scammers won’t
    • DO watch out for urgent or threatening language
      • Scammers often say things like “act now” or “your account is in danger.” These scare tactics are designed to make you respond quickly. Legitimate organizations don’t use pressure tactics
    • Do NOT send money or gift cards
      • Scammers may ask for payment via gift cards, wire transfers, or apps like CashApp. Real organizations don’t ask for payments in these ways
    • DO verify with official numbers
      • If someone claims to be from your bank or government agency, hang up and call back using the official number from their website, NOT the number they provide
  • Practice Responsible Sharing
  • Fact-check before sharing
    • It is great to share resources or tips, but confirm they are accurate to avoid spreading misinformation. Sharing verified information helps build trust within the community
